# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 1.2

Status: Frozen Orientation Document at `spec-v1.0`

---

# Why This Book Exists

We are not writing another textbook.

There are already excellent textbooks, courses, papers, and lectures.

The world does not need another long explanation of recurrent neural networks.

The world needs more people who are curious enough to want to learn them.

This book exists for that reason.

---

# Curiosity Before Knowledge

Information rarely creates curiosity.

Curiosity creates the desire for information.

Readers should first experience uncertainty.

Only then should they receive language.

Every important concept is earned.

Never delivered.

---

# Science Is Human

Science is not a collection of facts.

It is a way of living with uncertainty.

A scientist is not someone who possesses answers.

A scientist is someone who asks questions carefully enough that reality can answer them.

That is the transformation this novella seeks.

---

# We Reject Performance

The manuscript should never try to impress.

Not with jargon.

Not with cleverness.

Not with complexity.

Not with length.

Readers should leave feeling capable.

Never intimidated.

---

# We Reject Unnecessary Length

Brevity is not the absence of ideas.

It is respect for the reader's attention.

The novella should be readable over two or three evenings and remain memorable for years.

Required manuscript range:

- 25,000–35,000 words
- approximately 100–120 printed pages

Every page must earn its place.

The book should end before its central ideas begin to feel repeated.

---

# Literature Changes Attention

Facts fade.

Arguments weaken.

Attention remains.

If this novella succeeds, readers will begin noticing different things.

They will observe before explaining.

Pause before concluding.

Design experiments before defending beliefs.

---

# NeuroAI Is a Language

The book is not about glorifying today's models.

NeuroAI is the language through which we explore a timeless question:

How do minds build models of reality?

The technologies will evolve.

The question will remain.

---

# The Promise

The novella should inspire readers to open textbooks, papers, and code with curiosity rather than fear.

It is a bridge, not a destination.

Its concision should make that bridge easier to cross.

---

# The Standard

The book should never feel rushed.

It should never feel padded.

Its brevity must come from concentration, not omission.

When scientific honesty requires space, preserve the science and cut elsewhere.

When the reader has already understood, stop explaining.

---

# Final Commitment

We are not trying to teach readers what to think.

We are trying to help them discover how to think.

We will say only what the story and the reader's transformation genuinely require.

Everything else is secondary.
