# WORLD_BIBLE.md

# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.1

Status: Frozen at `spec-v1.0`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document defines the physical, emotional, and conceptual world of
*The Maze That Remembered*.

The world is not built to impress.

It is built to cultivate careful observation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Design Philosophy

The Institute exists slightly outside ordinary time.

It is neither futuristic nor historical.

Readers should never ask:

"What year is this?"

Instead they should ask:

"What kind of thinking happens here?"

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Atmosphere

The Institute evokes:

-   quiet
-   patience
-   craftsmanship
-   curiosity
-   reflection

It should feel closer to a monastery or workshop than a university or
technology company.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Material Palette

The world is tactile.

Recurring materials:

-   stone
-   wood
-   paper
-   glass
-   brass
-   iron
-   cloth
-   water

Everything ages gracefully.

Nothing feels disposable.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Major Locations

## The Courtyard

The birthplace of observation.

The crow first appears here.

The novella also returns here at the end.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Garden

Where patterns become visible.

Represents:

-   slow change
-   memory
-   hidden change over time

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Workshop

Where ideas become mechanisms.

Represents implementation rather than theory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Library

An archive of thinking rather than facts.

Books require interpretation just as data does.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Maze

The central metaphor.

The maze does not change.

The traveler does.

It gradually becomes a model of internal state and scientific thinking.

The maze has no agency, memory, hidden technology, or magical property.
It is physically unchanged in Chapter 15. The title names the model
remembered by Elias, not a maze that literally stores experience.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## The Observatory

Appears sparingly.

Represents perspective rather than astronomy.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Technology

Technology is intentionally understated.

Avoid:

-   futuristic interfaces
-   unnecessary screens
-   AI aesthetics

Scientific ideas should remain independent of contemporary hardware.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Time

No explicit year.

No social media.

No current political events.

The manuscript should age well.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Soundscape

Readers should repeatedly encounter:

-   footsteps
-   turning pages
-   rain
-   birds
-   wind
-   silence

Silence is an active part of the world.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Light

Natural light dominates.

Morning.

Afternoon.

Rain-filtered windows.

Changing light quietly marks the passage of time.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Educational Function

Each location teaches a mode of thinking.

Courtyard → Observation

Garden → Memory

Workshop → Mechanism

Library → Representation

Maze → Internal Models

Observatory → Perspective

No location should duplicate another's conceptual role.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# World Rules

The Institute never explains itself.

Characters behave as though everything is ordinary.

Readers discover meaning gradually.

Mystery comes from familiarity withheld,

not artificial secrets.

No major location may be added during Draft One. Scenes use only the
courtyard, garden, workshop, library, maze, and observatory.

The "bridge" in Chapter 12 is conceptual language, not a literal place
or a promoted symbol.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Forbidden Directions

The world must never become:

-   a magic school
-   a secret government laboratory
-   a Silicon Valley startup
-   a dystopian institution
-   a puzzle-box mystery

It remains one thing:

A place where people learn to think carefully.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Compass

Whenever describing the world ask:

Does this detail sharpen the reader's attention?

If not,

remove it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# One Sentence

The world exists to teach observation before explanation.
