# SYMBOL_BIBLE.md

# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.1

Status: Frozen at `spec-v1.0`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document defines every recurring symbol in the manuscript and how
its meaning evolves.

Symbols are never decorative.

They accumulate meaning as the reader changes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Symbol Philosophy

Readers should not think:

"This symbolizes..."

They should think:

"This feels different now."

Meaning grows through recurrence.

Never through explanation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Symbol Classes

Three primary symbols carry the complete arc of the novella:

-   The Crow
-   The Notebook
-   The Maze

Every primary symbol follows four stages:

1.  Object
2.  Tool
3.  Concept
4.  Mirror

No primary symbol skips stages.

The clock, library, garden, rain, light, stone, wood, glass, and silence
are supporting motifs. They must deepen when repeated, but they are not
required to perform the four-stage arc or return in the ending.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Crow

## Four-Stage Evolution

Object: A crow choosing among spoons. Nothing more.

Tool: Its behavior exposes Elias's projection and teaches him to
separate observation from explanation.

Concept: Its recurrence carries the limits of observation and the need
for provisional explanations.

Mirror: At the end, Elias responds to the same crow with questions
rather than certainty. The crow reveals his transformation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Meaning

Reality does not owe us explanations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Notebook

## Four-Stage Evolution

Object: A bound collection of blank paper.

Tool: A place to record observations, select useful history, and trace
relationships.

Concept: A provisional model organized increasingly by questions rather
than answers.

Mirror: Its remaining blank pages show that Elias no longer treats
knowledge as completion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Meaning

Good science leaves room for future revision.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Maze

## Four-Stage Evolution

Object: A physical obstacle and environment.

Tool: A setting for exposing the effects of history, memory, and hidden
state.

Concept: A representation of state space and thinking itself.

Mirror: The unchanged maze feels different because Elias now carries a
different model of it.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Meaning

Learning changes the traveler more than the path.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Clock

Represents:

Movement

↓

Intervention

↓

Causal role

↓

Partial explanation

Removing one gear teaches more than watching every gear move.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Library

Evolution:

Books

↓

Knowledge

↓

Representations

↓

Models

Knowledge requires interpretation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Garden

Evolution:

Plants

↓

Patterns

↓

Slow change

↓

Hidden change

Important transformations are often invisible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Rain

Never symbolizes sadness.

It teaches attention.

Readers learn to notice environments, not just events.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Light

Represents changing perspective.

As Elias learns, different things become visible.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Stone

Continuity.

Questions outlive individuals.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Wood

Construction.

Ideas are built.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Glass

Visibility is not understanding.

Transparency alone never explains a system.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Silence

Silence is an active symbol.

Different silences carry different meanings:

-   uncertainty
-   observation
-   reflection
-   satisfaction

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Symbol Density

No scene should actively develop more than two symbols.

Too many symbols weaken each other.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Recurrence Rule

Every return must deepen meaning.

Never repeat a symbol merely to remind readers it exists.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Return

The crow, notebook, and maze must return in Chapter 15.

They develop in separate scenes so that no scene actively develops more
than two symbols.

Not as nostalgia.

As conceptual completion.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Test

Ask:

What new layer of meaning has the reader earned since the last
appearance?

If the answer is "none,"

remove the recurrence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# One Sentence

The symbols change because the reader changes.
