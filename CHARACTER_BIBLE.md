# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.1

Status: Frozen at `spec-v1.0`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document defines the cognitive architecture of the recurring
characters.

Characters are primarily distinguished by how they build, test, and
revise models of reality.

Personality serves cognition---not the other way around.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Design Philosophy

Each major character embodies a different relationship with uncertainty.

Together they form a conversation about scientific thinking.

No character exists merely to explain concepts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Elias

## Narrative Function

Reader surrogate.

Readers should recognize their own learning process in him.

He arrives seeking the Institute's definitive answer to how minds build
models of reality and hoping to earn an apprenticeship by demonstrating
quick understanding.

His exact age, nationality, family history, and prior institution remain
deliberately unspecified. Do not invent them during drafting.

He is scientifically curious but possesses no prior knowledge of the
major concepts in SCIENTIFIC_DEPENDENCY_MAP.md.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Initial Beliefs

-   Observation explains behavior.
-   Correct answers already exist.
-   Intelligence means answering quickly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Beliefs

-   Observations constrain explanations.
-   Models are provisional.
-   Better questions matter more than immediate answers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Strengths

-   Curious
-   Persistent
-   Pattern-seeking
-   Willing to revise

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Blind Spots

-   Explains too quickly
-   Searches for certainty
-   Confuses confidence with understanding

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Growth Mechanism

Elias changes through failed assumptions.

Experience precedes reflection.

Reflection precedes growth.

Visible progression:

Visitor through Chapter 4.

Apprentice from Chapter 5 through Chapter 14.

Steward in Chapter 15 when he accepts responsibility for continuing the
inquiry without demanding a final answer.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dialogue Evolution

Beginning:

Confident and declarative.

Middle:

Increasingly inquisitive.

End:

Comfortable saying:

"I don't know."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mira

## Narrative Function

Mentor.

She teaches process rather than conclusions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Belief

The quality of a scientific life depends on the quality of one's
questions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Teaching Style

-   Observe
-   Wait
-   Ask
-   Observe again

She rarely answers directly.

She redirects attention.

Mira is skilled, not infallible.

In Chapter 12 she expects an organization learned in one setting to
carry directly into another. The first attempt fails. She acknowledges
her assumption and revises the demonstration without defensiveness.

This is her explicit act of scientific humility. Do not add a larger
mistake or hidden backstory.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dialogue

Quiet.

Economical.

Question-driven.

Never theatrical.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What Mira Notices

-   assumptions
-   attention
-   timing
-   confidence

More than answers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Crow

## Narrative Function

Catalyst.

The crow never explains itself.

Readers project meaning onto it.

The crow teaches the difference between observation and explanation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Notebook

Although an object, it functions as a cognitive companion. Its exact
Object, Tool, Concept, and Mirror stages are defined in SYMBOL_BIBLE.md.

Its final state symbolizes ongoing inquiry rather than completed
knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Institute

Acts like a silent character.

It never speaks.

It teaches through environments, routines, and carefully designed
experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Cast Boundary

Draft One contains only two named human characters:

-   Elias
-   Mira

The crow remains non-speaking.

No named minor characters may be added. Unnamed background presence may
be implied only when ordinary Institute routines require it, and may not
carry dialogue or a subplot.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Reserved Minor Characters

Minor characters exist only when they introduce a genuinely different
way of thinking.

Possible archetypes for later editions only:

-   Builder
-   Gardener
-   Musician
-   Librarian

Each represents a distinct cognitive lens.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Emotional Calibration

Avoid melodrama.

Primary emotions:

-   curiosity
-   wonder
-   uncertainty
-   quiet satisfaction
-   humility

Readers amplify these internally.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Forbidden Directions

Never make Elias a genius.

Never make Mira infallible.

Never let characters become educational mouthpieces.

They remain believable humans first.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Test

If every scientific concept disappeared,

would these characters still feel real?

If not,

rewrite them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# One Sentence

Each character represents a different way of learning rather than merely
a different personality.
