# CHARACTER_BIBLE.md

# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.0

Status: Frozen Before Draft One

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document defines the cognitive architecture of the recurring
characters.

Characters are primarily distinguished by how they build, test, and
revise models of reality.

Personality serves cognition---not the other way around.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Design Philosophy

Each major character embodies a different relationship with uncertainty.

Together they form a conversation about scientific thinking.

No character exists merely to explain concepts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Elias

## Narrative Function

Reader surrogate.

Readers should recognize their own learning process in him.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Initial Beliefs

-   Observation explains behavior.
-   Correct answers already exist.
-   Intelligence means answering quickly.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Final Beliefs

-   Observations constrain explanations.
-   Models are provisional.
-   Better questions matter more than immediate answers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Strengths

-   Curious
-   Persistent
-   Pattern-seeking
-   Willing to revise

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Blind Spots

-   Explains too quickly
-   Searches for certainty
-   Confuses confidence with understanding

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Growth Mechanism

Elias changes through failed assumptions.

Experience precedes reflection.

Reflection precedes growth.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dialogue Evolution

Beginning:

Confident and declarative.

Middle:

Increasingly inquisitive.

End:

Comfortable saying:

"I don't know."

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Mira

## Narrative Function

Mentor.

She teaches process rather than conclusions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Core Belief

The quality of a scientific life depends on the quality of one's
questions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Teaching Style

-   Observe
-   Wait
-   Ask
-   Observe again

She rarely answers directly.

She redirects attention.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dialogue

Quiet.

Economical.

Question-driven.

Never theatrical.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## What Mira Notices

-   assumptions
-   attention
-   timing
-   confidence

More than answers.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Crow

## Narrative Function

Catalyst.

The crow never explains itself.

Readers project meaning onto it.

The crow teaches the difference between observation and explanation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Notebook

Although an object, it functions as a cognitive companion.

Evolution:

Observations

↓

Patterns

↓

Questions

↓

Blank pages

Its final state symbolizes ongoing inquiry rather than completed
knowledge.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# The Institute

Acts like a silent character.

It never speaks.

It teaches through environments, routines, and carefully designed
experiences.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Minor Characters

Minor characters exist only when they introduce a genuinely different
way of thinking.

Possible archetypes:

-   Builder
-   Gardener
-   Musician
-   Librarian

Each represents a distinct cognitive lens.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Relationships

## Elias ↔ Mira

Begins as student and mentor.

Ends as two scientists asking better questions together.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Elias ↔ Institute

Visitor

↓

Apprentice

↓

Steward

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Emotional Calibration

Avoid melodrama.

Primary emotions:

-   curiosity
-   wonder
-   uncertainty
-   quiet satisfaction
-   humility

Readers amplify these internally.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Forbidden Directions

Never make Elias a genius.

Never make Mira infallible.

Never let characters become educational mouthpieces.

They remain believable humans first.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Test

If every scientific concept disappeared,

would these characters still feel real?

If not,

rewrite them.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# One Sentence

Each character represents a different way of learning rather than merely
a different personality.
