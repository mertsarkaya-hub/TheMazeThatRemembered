# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.1

Status: Frozen Before Draft One

---

# Purpose

Each chapter is defined by the transformation it performs in the reader—not by plot alone.

The manuscript is a literary scientific novella.

Every chapter must be readable in a single sitting.

---

# Global Length Targets

Total manuscript:

- 25,000–35,000 words
- Approximately 100–120 printed pages

Chapter target:

- 1,700–2,200 words

Interlude:

- 800–1,200 words

These are design constraints, not quotas.

Never expand a chapter simply to reach a number.

---

# Universal Blueprint

Every chapter must answer:

- What does the reader currently believe?
- Which assumption breaks?
- Which scientific concept is earned?
- What emotional shift occurs?
- Which symbols deepen?
- What question launches the next chapter?

Only one major cognitive transformation per chapter.

# Compression Rule

If a chapter completes its transformation in fewer words,

stop.

Never dilute a completed transformation.

---

# Completion Test

A chapter is finished when:

- the transformation is complete
- the next question naturally emerges
- no paragraph can be removed without weakening the experience

---

# Final Principle

A chapter should end while the reader still wants one more page.
