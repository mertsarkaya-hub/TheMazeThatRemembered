# Purpose

This document defines the conceptual dependency graph of the manuscript.

It functions as the project's cognitive compiler.

Every scientific concept must be introduced only after the reader has
earned the prerequisites required to understand it.

If a dependency is violated, the manuscript fails to compile.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Core Principle

Understanding is cumulative.

Readers do not collect concepts.

They reorganize mental models.

Each chapter performs one structural update to that internal model.

# Dependency Rules

## Observation

Requires no prerequisites.

Reader learns that observation differs from explanation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Hypothesis

Depends on Observation.

Reader learns multiple explanations can fit identical observations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## History Matters

Depends on Observation and Hypothesis.

Reader discovers that present behavior depends on previous events.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Memory

Depends on History.

Memory preserves relevant information rather than everything.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Hidden State

Depends on Memory.

Internal state summarizes useful history.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Recurrent Computation

Depends on Hidden State.

Thinking becomes continuous state updating.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Representation

Depends on Recurrent Computation.

Internal states encode useful information.

Representations are distributed rather than localized.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Representational Geometry

Depends on Representation.

Meaning emerges through relationships among representations.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Representational Similarity

Depends on Geometry.

Different systems can be compared by relational structure.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dynamics

Depends on RSA.

Representations evolve through time.

Snapshots are insufficient.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Dynamic Similarity

Depends on Dynamics.

Trajectories become more informative than positions.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Intervention

Depends on Dynamic understanding.

Observation alone cannot establish mechanism.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Transfer

Depends on Representation and Intervention.

Useful representations support multiple tasks.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Mechanism

Depends on Transfer.

Behavior does not uniquely identify computation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Interpretability

Depends on Mechanism.

Visibility is not explanation.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Scientific Thinking

Depends on every previous concept.

Science continually improves models through evidence.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Compiler Errors

Reject the manuscript if:

-   terminology appears before necessity
-   prerequisites are missing
-   future concepts are referenced prematurely
-   concepts duplicate one another
-   earlier mental models are contradicted rather than extended

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Reinforcement Rule

Every concept follows four phases:

Need

↓

Introduction

↓

Application

↓

Integration

Definitions are introduced once.

Understanding is reinforced repeatedly through use.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Validation Checklist

For every chapter ask:

-   What does the reader currently believe?
-   Which assumption fails?
-   Which concept is earned?
-   What future concepts now become possible?

If any answer is unclear, revise the dependency chain.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Principle

Readers should repeatedly think:

"Of course."

Never:

"Where did this come from?"

That is the definition of a successfully compiled manuscript.
