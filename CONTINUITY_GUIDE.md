# CONTINUITY_GUIDE.md

# The Maze That Remembered

Version: 2.1

Status: Frozen Protocol at `spec-v1.0`

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Purpose

This document preserves continuity across the manuscript.

It tracks not only events, but the evolution of characters, symbols,
scientific concepts, emotional tone, and the reader's mental model.

Continuity exists to create coherence rather than mere consistency.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Types of Continuity

Maintain six independent threads:

-   Narrative continuity
-   Character continuity
-   Scientific continuity
-   Symbolic continuity
-   Emotional continuity
-   Reader continuity

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Reader Continuity

At every chapter record:

-   Current mental model
-   Assumptions held
-   Assumptions broken
-   Concepts earned
-   Questions carried forward

The reader is treated as an evolving character.

The canonical planned states are stored in CHAPTER_BLUEPRINTS.md.
During drafting, evidence for the actual states is recorded in the
chapter QA report outside the frozen specification corpus.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Character Continuity

## Elias

Track:

-   confidence
-   curiosity
-   patience
-   quality of questions
-   comfort with uncertainty

Growth must be gradual.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

## Mira

Track:

-   questioning style
-   silence
-   consistency
-   reader's perception of her

Mira changes very little.

The reader's understanding of Mira changes.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Symbol Continuity

For every recurring symbol maintain:

-   first appearance
-   latest appearance
-   current meaning
-   next planned evolution

Symbols must deepen.

Never merely repeat.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Scientific Continuity

Track every major concept through four stages:

Need

↓

Introduction

↓

Application

↓

Integration

Concepts should never disappear for long stretches.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Emotional Continuity

Primary emotions:

-   curiosity
-   uncertainty
-   recognition
-   wonder
-   humility
-   quiet satisfaction

Avoid abrupt emotional shifts.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Callback Matrix

Every chapter should intentionally reconnect with earlier ideas.

Late chapters reward attentive readers through conceptual callbacks
rather than nostalgia.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Analogy Tracker

Every analogy has:

-   purpose
-   strengths
-   limitations
-   retirement point

Retire analogies before they become misleading.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Revision Checklist

Whenever a chapter changes ask:

-   Does this affect foreshadowing?
-   Character growth?
-   Symbol evolution?
-   Scientific dependencies?
-   Emotional pacing?
-   Reader transformation?

Update every affected section.

"Update" means update the manuscript and its external production notes.
Do not edit this guide or any other frozen specification file during
drafting.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Global Test

Removing any chapter should noticeably weaken:

-   the narrative
-   the scientific progression
-   the symbolic arc
-   the reader's transformation

If not,

the manuscript lacks continuity.

------------------------------------------------------------------------

# Final Principle

Continuity is the gradual growth of one coherent mind across the entire
novella.
